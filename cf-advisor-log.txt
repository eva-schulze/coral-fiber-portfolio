Client: Shelter the Sharks
Question: What about their GDPR compliance? They collect data from homeless youth.
Response:
**⚠️ HIGHEST PRIORITY: SENSITIVE DATA + FUNDER DEADLINE IN 45 DAYS**

Working with homeless youth data triggers heightened GDPR/AVG scrutiny. Funders (especially government or large foundations) will audit this. You must fix this *before* the financial submission.

---

## Immediate Actions

- **Conduct a data audit this week:** Map every place you store youth data (spreadsheets, email, paper forms, social media). Identify: names, ages, addresses, health/trauma info, photos, contact details. List who has access.
- **Create a basic Data Processing Agreement (DPA)** for any third-party tools you use (Google Workspace, Moneybird, even WhatsApp if you message beneficiaries). Without signed DPAs, you're non-compliant and liable for €7,500+ fines.
- **Implement consent forms immediately** for all new data collection. For existing youth: retrospectively document consent or delete data you can't justify. Document this deletion in writing.

---

## 30-Day Plan

- **Week 1-2:** Draft a Privacy Policy (in Dutch) explaining what data you collect, why, how long you keep it, and youth rights. Post it visibly.
- **Week 2:** Audit consent—youth (or guardians) must have explicitly agreed to data use. If they haven't, stop processing that data.
- **Week 3:** Implement basic security: encrypted file storage (SharePoint with EU residency confirmed, not Dropbox), access logs, password policies. Train staff on data handling.
- **Week 4:** Prepare a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) summary for your funder showing you've assessed risks to vulnerable youth.

---

## Risk Flags

- **Photo/video consent is missing:** Images of homeless youth are especially sensitive. You need explicit, documented consent for *each* use (internal reports vs. social media vs. fundraising materials). Verbal consent isn't enough.
- **Funder rejection risk:** Dutch government funders (gemeente, provinciaal) now require GDPR compliance verification. Missing DPAs or consent will trigger funding hold or clawback.
- **ANBI status threatened:** If your AVG violation is reported, ANBI registration can be revoked. Your funder likely has audit rights to inspect your data practices.

**Next step:** Get a Dutch data protection specialist (€500–1,000 for emergency audit) *before* submitting to funder. This is cheaper than funder rejection.
